# fdu_030

## Round 1
Create a premium long-scroll single-file `src/index.html` for a **hospitality or travel page** called **Letter Retreat**.
Concept: a creator newsletter platform reimagined as a retreat-style hospitality experience for founders who want deep-focus writing residencies, editorial workshops, and quiet community.
Audience: design-conscious founders, independent writers, and creative operators who want premium retreat storytelling with real booking intent.
Style direction: **Organic Warm** with references to human-centered hospitality brands, warm editorial product pages, and refined founder-focused storytelling.
Component language: soft rounded modules, editorial schedule panels, warm testimonial blocks, refined booking moments, and premium CTA sections.
Color direction: oat, sand, clay, espresso, muted sage, and warm amber or terracotta accents with grounded contrast.
Typography direction: friendly but premium display headings paired with warm contemporary body text.
Motion direction: gentle, human, and polished with soft reveal motion and tasteful state changes.
Build a complete page with these sections: navbar, hero booking moment, retreat overview, residency experiences, workshop schedule, accommodation highlights, testimonials, FAQ, booking CTA, footer.
Required interactions: hover states, active states, carousel, counters, FAQ accordion.
The page should feel like a thoughtfully branded founder retreat rather than a generic boutique getaway page.

## Round 2
Deepen the layout and content density.
The hero should feel calm, literary, and destination-driven, with a clear invitation to book a focused writing retreat experience.
Add believable copy around editorial workshops, writing cabins, feedback circles, quiet scheduling, founder networking, residency pacing, and premium retreat amenities.
Use immersive atmosphere, booking intent, and sensory detail without falling into generic retreat clichés.
Make the page alternate between emotional destination storytelling and practical residency structure so the experience feels both aspirational and bookable.
Keep enough vertical depth for a long full-page screenshot, with multiple strong section transitions and no thin filler sections.

## Round 3
Polish all interaction states and compliance details.
Hover and active states should read clearly across booking controls, cards, and CTA modules.
Any carousel-style accommodation or schedule highlights should feel intentional rather than gimmicky, counters should animate with clean timing, and the FAQ accordion should feel polished and easy to scan.
Ensure the layout is responsive for desktop and tablet widths while preserving the warm literary hospitality identity.
Keep all CSS and JS inline inside `index.html`.
Do not use React, Vue, Svelte, jQuery, or any build step.
Do not reference local images, local fonts, local CSS, or local JS.
Use remote imagery only if needed and inline SVG icons.
Return only the final `src/index.html` content with no explanation.

## Round 4
Final refinement pass.
The result should feel calm, art-directed, and genuinely retreat-ready while still making booking feel easy.
Avoid wellness-retreat clichés, vague community claims, and travel-template sameness.
Ensure enough depth for a long full-page screenshot and visible interactive states throughout.
Return one complete self-contained `index.html`.
