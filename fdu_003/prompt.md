# fdu_003

## Round 1
Create a premium long-scroll single-file `src/index.html` for an **ecommerce product page** called **Velvet Leaf**.
Concept: a luxury tea subscription brand for young premium consumers who want ritual, taste discovery, and beautifully designed recurring delivery.
Audience: young premium consumers who appreciate design, wellness rituals, gifting, and elevated lifestyle products.
Style direction: **Minimalist** with references to modern editorial commerce, Aesop-like restraint, and high-end lifestyle product photography.
Component language: minimal cards, refined product modules, premium plan selector, soft modal CTA, understated navigation.
Color direction: warm porcelain, deep tea green, matte black, muted brass, soft stone neutrals.
Typography direction: elegant serif-led headlines with restrained sans-serif support text.
Motion direction: smooth, subtle, calm, tactile.
Build a complete page with these sections: navbar, hero, product story, tea collection highlights, subscription plans, ritual section, sourcing section, testimonials, FAQ, final CTA, footer.
Required interactions: hover states, pricing toggle, modal CTA, smooth scrolling, navbar blur.
The page should feel like a real premium DTC brand and not like a generic Shopify theme.

## Round 2
Increase content richness and product depth.
Use a refined hero with strong product storytelling, a premium packshot composition, and immediate subscription value communication.
Create a pricing toggle that switches between monthly and quarterly plans.
Add flavor collection cards for different tea profiles with believable tasting notes and ritual recommendations.
Build a sourcing section that highlights origin, craftsmanship, and sustainability without sounding generic.
Include testimonials that feel specific and premium.
Use section pacing that alternates between editorial spaciousness and conversion-focused detail.
Make the modal CTA feel intentional, branded, and elegant rather than intrusive.
Keep enough vertical content for a long full-page screenshot.

## Round 3
Polish interaction and browsing behavior.
Add subtle hover behavior for product cards, links, plan options, and CTA buttons.
Use a soft blurred navbar transition while scrolling.
Smooth scrolling should feel polished and premium.
The pricing toggle should visibly change plan content and pricing.
The CTA modal should open cleanly, be easy to close, and remain visually aligned with the brand.
Ensure responsive behavior for desktop and tablet.
Keep all CSS and JS inline in `index.html`.
No local assets, no local libraries, no frameworks, no build step.
Use remote imagery only if needed and inline SVG icons.
Return only the final HTML for `src/index.html`.

## Round 4
Final quality pass.
Make the page feel restrained, luxurious, and commerce-ready.
Avoid clutter, loud gradients, filler copy, and generic ecommerce tropes.
Ensure the page has enough detail, motion, and interaction variety to feel complete in a long screenshot.
Return one self-contained `index.html` only.
