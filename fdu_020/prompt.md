# fdu_020

## Round 1
Create a premium long-scroll single-file `src/index.html` for a **hospitality or travel page** called **Nocturne House**.
Concept: a boutique hotel booking page for creative professionals who want atmosphere, architecture, quiet luxury, and a cinematic nighttime city stay.
Audience: creative professionals, art directors, photographers, and founders who respond to design-led hospitality experiences.
Style direction: **Dark Mode** with references to premium boutique hotel sites, cinematic hospitality launches, and art-directed dark editorial travel pages.
Component language: layered dark cards, contrast-rich booking modules, elegant gallery panels, tabbed experience sections, and premium CTA blocks.
Color direction: near-black, graphite, deep indigo, smoky gray, and restrained gold or silver accent highlights.
Typography direction: sharp modern headings mixed with editorial sophistication and highly readable body text.
Motion direction: controlled, cinematic, premium, and precise rather than flashy.
Build a complete page with these sections: navbar, hero booking moment, room collection, signature spaces, gallery or suite carousel, guest experience details, testimonials, FAQ, booking CTA, footer.
Required interactions: hover states, tabs, accordion, scroll reveal, smooth scrolling.
The page should feel like a serious boutique hotel brand rather than a generic dark travel template.

## Round 2
Deepen the layout and content density.
The hero should feel cinematic and moody, with a strong sense of place, layered suite imagery, and immediate reservation intent.
Add believable copy around suite types, architectural character, late-night amenities, local neighborhood stories, concierge experiences, and premium booking reassurance.
Use immersive atmosphere, booking intent, and sensory detail without falling into generic travel clichés.
Make the room and experience sections feel curated and art-directed rather than like a standard booking engine skin.
Keep enough vertical depth for a long full-page screenshot, with multiple strong section transitions and no thin filler sections.

## Round 3
Polish all interaction states and compliance details.
Tabs should feel crisp and clearly stateful, accordions should expand smoothly with strong contrast, and scroll reveal motion should guide attention without slowing the page down.
Smooth scrolling should make anchor navigation feel premium rather than ornamental.
Ensure the layout is responsive for desktop and tablet widths while preserving the cinematic nighttime tone.
Keep all CSS and JS inline inside `index.html`.
Do not use React, Vue, Svelte, jQuery, or any build step.
Do not reference local images, local fonts, local CSS, or local JS.
Use remote imagery only if needed and inline SVG icons.
Return only the final `src/index.html` content with no explanation.

## Round 4
Final refinement pass.
The result should feel atmospheric, luxurious, and reservation-ready while still being easy to navigate.
Avoid nightclub aesthetics, generic luxury buzzwords, and empty booking widgets.
Ensure enough depth for a long full-page screenshot and visible interactive states throughout.
Return one complete self-contained `index.html`.
