# fdu_023

## Round 1
Create a premium long-scroll single-file `src/index.html` for an **ecommerce product page** called **Altitude Carry**.
Concept: a sustainable luggage brand aimed at enterprise buyers and executive travelers who want premium mobility, systems thinking, and polished team travel gear.
Audience: enterprise buyers, executive travelers, and procurement-minded design lovers who want premium commerce with strong product logic.
Style direction: **Gradient Mesh** with references to premium future-facing product launches, polished travel gear campaigns, and vibrant commerce storytelling.
Component language: glow-backed cards, layered product frames, luminous stat chips, polished bundle selectors, and premium commerce CTA modules.
Color direction: deep plum, electric blue, refined violet, cool cyan, and restrained coral accents blended through premium gradient transitions.
Typography direction: bold display-driven headings with smooth rounded sans-serif support text.
Motion direction: fluid, expressive, high-energy, and polished without becoming chaotic.
Build a complete page with these sections: navbar, hero, product family highlights, feature grid, materials story, bundle or plan selector, travel scenarios, testimonials, FAQ, CTA, footer.
Required interactions: hover states, pricing toggle, modal CTA, smooth scrolling, navbar blur.
The page should feel like a next-generation premium travel brand rather than a hypey gadget storefront.

## Round 2
Deepen the layout and content density.
The hero should mix sharp product renders, mesh-lit energy, and a clear premium travel-system message with strong purchase momentum.

Make the benefits, bundle logic, and proof points persuasive and commerce-ready rather than template-like.
Use richer section pacing so the page balances visual energy with clear product logic and practical buying confidence.
Keep enough vertical depth for a long full-page screenshot, with multiple strong section transitions and no thin filler sections.

## Round 3

Make the pricing toggle visibly change bundle framing or purchase options, and ensure the modal CTA opens and closes cleanly without feeling intrusive.

Ensure the layout is responsive for desktop and tablet widths while preserving the premium commerce feel.
Keep all CSS and JS inline inside `index.html`.
Do not use React, Vue, Svelte, jQuery, or any build step.
Do not reference local images, local fonts, local CSS, or local JS.
Use remote imagery only if needed and inline SVG icons.
Return only the final `src/index.html` content with no explanation.

## Round 4
Final refinement pass.
The result should feel premium, future-facing, and commerce-ready while still staying grounded in believable product value.
