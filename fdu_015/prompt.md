# fdu_015

## Round 1
Create a premium long-scroll single-file `src/index.html` for a **mobile app landing page** called **LumaLingo**.
Concept: a language learning app for young premium consumers who want immersive speaking practice, daily habit systems, and beautifully designed mobile progress loops.
Audience: young premium consumers, students, and ambitious self-improvers who expect premium mobile design and believable learning motivation.
Style direction: **Gradient Mesh** with references to futuristic consumer app launches, premium learning startups, and vibrant mesh-gradient hero systems.
Component language: glow-backed cards, layered phone mockup frames, luminous stat chips, polished progress modules, and premium subscription CTAs.
Color direction: deep plum, electric blue, vivid violet, cyan, and soft coral accents blended through refined gradients.
Typography direction: bold display-driven headings with smooth rounded sans-serif support text.
Motion direction: fluid, expressive, high-energy, and polished without becoming chaotic.
Build a complete page with these sections: sticky navbar, hero, phone mockup showcase, lesson journey, streak and progress modules, social proof, subscription plans, testimonials, FAQ, footer.
Required interactions: hover states, pricing toggle, modal CTA, smooth scrolling, navbar blur.
The page should feel like a premium consumer app launch rather than a generic edtech landing page.

## Round 2
Deepen the layout and content density.
The hero should show a vibrant phone-led composition, a clear learning promise, and immediate habit-building value with dual CTAs.
Add believable copy around speaking drills, spaced repetition, travel scenarios, pronunciation coaching, streak systems, social accountability, and premium subscription framing.
Make the phone UI modules feel believable, product-specific, and polished rather than like generic placeholder screens.
Use richer section pacing so the page alternates between energetic app storytelling, proof of learning progress, and premium conversion moments.
Keep enough vertical depth for a long full-page screenshot, with multiple strong section transitions and no thin filler sections.

## Round 3
Polish all interaction states and compliance details.
Make the pricing toggle visibly change plan details, and ensure the modal CTA opens and closes cleanly without feeling intrusive.
Smooth scrolling and the navbar blur transition should feel polished and intentionally branded.
Ensure the layout is responsive for desktop and tablet widths while preserving the vibrant premium app feel.
Keep all CSS and JS inline inside `index.html`.
Do not use React, Vue, Svelte, jQuery, or any build step.
Do not reference local images, local fonts, local CSS, or local JS.
Use remote imagery only if needed and inline SVG icons.
Return only the final `src/index.html` content with no explanation.

## Round 4
Final refinement pass.
The result should feel energetic, premium, and conversion-oriented while still being believable as a real product launch page.
Avoid childish mascot energy, gamification clichés, and bland app-store-copy wording.
Ensure enough depth for a long full-page screenshot and visible interactive states throughout.
Return one complete self-contained `index.html`.
