# fdu_010

## Round 1
Create a premium long-scroll single-file `src/index.html` for a **hospitality or travel page** called **Ember Table**.
Concept: a restaurant tasting menu launch page that frames the dining experience like an immersive destination with chapters, pairings, atmosphere, and limited reservations.
Audience: developers and operators with strong design taste, plus urban diners who appreciate concept-driven hospitality and elevated sensory storytelling.
Style direction: **Glassmorphism** with references to luxury hospitality pages, cinematic translucent UI, and high-end immersive brand sites.
Component language: translucent panels, blurred overlays, refined segmented controls, elevated booking widgets, layered content cards, and premium reservation moments.
Color direction: smoky charcoal, soft ivory, misted champagne, silver-blue translucency, and muted ember accents.
Typography direction: editorial serif-led headings paired with sophisticated sans-serif body copy.
Motion direction: smooth, ambient, and cinematic with soft depth changes and elegant transitions.
Build a complete page with these sections: navbar, hero reservation moment, tasting chapters, chef philosophy, pairings carousel, atmosphere gallery, guest notes, FAQ accordion, booking CTA, footer.
Required interactions: hover states, active states, carousel, counters, FAQ accordion.
The page should feel like a high-end hospitality launch, not a generic restaurant directory page.

## Round 2
Deepen the layout and content density.
The hero should feel cinematic and reservation-driven, with a premium headline, soft layered imagery, and immediate tasting-menu intent.
Add believable copy around seasonal courses, ingredient provenance, pacing of the meal, beverage pairings, chef notes, seating cadence, and reservation scarcity.
Use immersive atmosphere, booking intent, and sensory detail without falling into generic restaurant clichés.
Give the page richer storytelling structure so the tasting experience unfolds like a sequence of chapters rather than disconnected sections.
Keep enough vertical depth for a long full-page screenshot, with multiple strong section transitions and no thin filler sections.

## Round 3
Polish all interaction states and compliance details.
Hover and active states should read clearly across booking controls, cards, and CTAs.
Carousel motion should feel elegant rather than gimmicky, counters should animate with clean timing, and the FAQ accordion should feel polished and easy to scan.
Ensure the layout is responsive for desktop and tablet widths while preserving the cinematic hospitality feel.
Keep all CSS and JS inline inside `index.html`.
Do not use React, Vue, Svelte, jQuery, or any build step.
Do not reference local images, local fonts, local CSS, or local JS.
Use remote imagery only if needed and inline SVG icons.
Return only the final `src/index.html` content with no explanation.

## Round 4
Final refinement pass.
The result should feel luxurious, sensory, and reservation-ready while still remaining usable and polished.
Avoid cheesy fine-dining clichés, stock foodie adjectives, and lifeless reservation modules.
Ensure enough depth for a long full-page screenshot and visible interactive states throughout.
Return one complete self-contained `index.html`.
