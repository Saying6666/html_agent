# fdu_006

## Round 1
Create a premium long-scroll single-file `src/index.html` for a **dashboard** called **LetterMoss**.
Concept: a creator newsletter platform for design-conscious founders who want audience growth, sponsorship health, editorial planning, and community retention in one calm operating system.
Audience: design-conscious founders, independent media operators, and creator-led startups that care about clarity, growth quality, and sustainable publishing systems.
Style direction: **Organic Warm** with references to human-centered SaaS brands, warm editorial product pages, and artisanal modern storytelling.
Component language: soft rounded modules, warm stat blocks, editorial side panels, polished data cards, and premium conversion sections.
Color direction: oat, sand, clay, espresso, muted sage, and warm amber accents; grounded, premium, and human rather than cold analytics blue.
Typography direction: friendly but premium display headings paired with warm contemporary body text.
Motion direction: gentle, human, and polished with soft reveal motion and tasteful state changes.
Build a complete page with these sections: sticky navbar, hero summary, KPI strip, subscriber health dashboard, sponsorship pipeline, editorial calendar, audience segments, pricing, FAQ, footer.
Required interactions: hover states, active states, carousel or rotating highlights, counters, FAQ accordion.
The page should feel like a premium founder tool with human warmth, not a bland analytics admin screen.

## Round 2
Deepen the layout and content density.
The hero should combine a warm product promise, credible growth metrics, a calm dashboard composition, and two strong call-to-action paths.
Add believable product copy around issue planning, referral loops, sponsorship forecasting, subscriber cohorts, churn signals, and creator workflow automation.
Use stronger visual hierarchy, richer section depth, premium module composition, and more intentional spacing rhythm throughout the page.
Let the dashboard areas feel operationally real with comparison cards, recent activity, segment snapshots, trend views, and creator-oriented summary moments rather than decorative charts alone.
Keep enough vertical depth for a long full-page screenshot, with multiple strong section transitions and no thin filler sections.

## Round 3
Polish all interaction states and compliance details.
Hover and active states should read clearly across cards, filters, buttons, and CTA modules.
Any carousel-style highlights should feel intentional rather than gimmicky, counters should animate with clean timing, and the FAQ accordion should feel polished and easy to scan.
Ensure the layout is responsive for desktop and tablet widths while preserving the warm brand identity.
Keep all CSS and JS inline inside `index.html`.
Do not use React, Vue, Svelte, jQuery, or any build step.
Do not reference local images, local fonts, local CSS, or local JS.
Use remote imagery only if needed and inline SVG icons.
Return only the final `src/index.html` content with no explanation.

## Round 4
Final refinement pass.
The result should feel calm, founder-friendly, and commercially credible while still looking like a polished modern product.
Avoid generic chart spam, startup buzzword filler, and empty analytics cards.
Ensure enough depth for a long full-page screenshot and visible interactive states throughout.
Return one complete self-contained `index.html`.
