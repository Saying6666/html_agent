# fdu_024

## Round 1
Create a premium long-scroll single-file `src/index.html` for a **portfolio site** called **CareForm Studio**.
Concept: a telehealth wellness app framed as a bold portfolio-style product case study for founders who want a distinctive health-tech brand narrative.
Audience: design-conscious founders, product teams, and creative directors who want health-tech storytelling with more personality and structural confidence.
Style direction: **Neo-Brutalism** with references to bold editorial tech posters, high-contrast interface systems, and playful brutalist layouts with premium discipline.
Component language: thick bordered cards, offset shadows, oversized labels, case-study modules, punchy CTA blocks, and tactile content panels.
Color direction: black and white foundations with curated accents such as acid yellow, tomato red, cobalt, and mint used with strong restraint.
Typography direction: assertive display typography with sturdy supporting sans-serif text and bold label systems.
Motion direction: snappy, tactile, playful, and clearly stateful.
Build a complete page with these sections: navbar, hero intro, selected work grid, product spotlight, care journey case study, process section, testimonials, FAQ accordion, contact/footer CTA.
Required interactions: hover states, tabs, accordion, scroll reveal, smooth scrolling.
The page should feel like a memorable premium case study rather than a novelty brutalist experiment.

## Round 2
Deepen the layout and content density.
The hero should introduce the product with strong attitude, bold labels, and a clear argument for trust, care continuity, and user-centered health design.
Add believable copy around care plans, check-in workflows, clinician matching, privacy-aware onboarding, wellness insights, and human-centered interaction design.
Make the story feel curated and case-study-led, not like a simple gallery of cards with weak context.
Use stronger narrative pacing between health outcomes, product proof, and portfolio storytelling so the page feels distinctive but credible.
Keep enough vertical depth for a long full-page screenshot, with multiple strong section transitions and no thin filler sections.

## Round 3
Polish all interaction states and compliance details.
Tabs should feel crisp and clearly stateful, accordions should expand smoothly with strong contrast, and scroll reveal motion should guide attention without slowing the page down.
Smooth scrolling should make anchor navigation feel premium rather than ornamental.
Ensure the layout is responsive for desktop and tablet widths while preserving the bold health-tech identity.
Keep all CSS and JS inline inside `index.html`.
Do not use React, Vue, Svelte, jQuery, or any build step.
Do not reference local images, local fonts, local CSS, or local JS.
Use remote imagery only if needed and inline SVG icons.
Return only the final `src/index.html` content with no explanation.

## Round 4
Final refinement pass.
The result should feel bold, trustworthy, and design-forward while still respecting the seriousness of healthcare-adjacent messaging.
Avoid cartoonish healthcare visuals, irresponsible medical claims, and style-over-substance layouts.
Ensure enough depth for a long full-page screenshot and visible interactive states throughout.
Return one complete self-contained `index.html`.
