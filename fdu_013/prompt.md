# fdu_013

## Round 1
Create a premium long-scroll single-file `src/index.html` for an **ecommerce product page** called **Waymark Atlas**.
Concept: a sustainable luggage brand for thoughtful travelers who want modular packing, durable materials, and premium design without disposable travel gear.
Audience: mid-market product teams and design-aware shoppers who appreciate utility, systems thinking, and premium commerce experiences.
Style direction: **Bento Grid** with references to Aceternity UI / Magic UI-inspired modular storytelling, premium launch pages, and structured product marketing grids.
Component language: bento cards, polished stat tiles, product mockup frames, modular CTA stacks, and commerce-ready comparison modules.
Color direction: deep navy, ink black, cool gray foundations with selective bright accents such as amber and muted lime.
Typography direction: strong geometric headings with crisp modern sans-serif body copy.
Motion direction: product-like, crisp, modular, and high-signal but still refined.
Build a complete page with these sections: navbar, hero, collection highlights, bento feature grid, travel use cases, materials and durability, bundle selector, reviews, FAQ, CTA, footer.
Required interactions: button hover states, button active states, counter animations, scroll reveal animations, sticky navbar transition on scroll.
The page should feel like a premium modern travel brand rather than a generic Shopify luggage page.

## Round 2
Deepen the layout and content density.
The hero should combine strong packshot storytelling, modular luggage composition, and a clear premium commerce message with immediate purchase intent.
Add believable copy around carry-on logic, packing systems, recycled shell materials, modular accessories, lifetime repair framing, and traveler workflow benefits.
Make the benefits, bundle logic, and proof points persuasive and commerce-ready rather than template-like.
Use a richer bento rhythm so every card contributes to product understanding, not just decoration.
Keep enough vertical depth for a long full-page screenshot, with multiple strong section transitions and no thin filler sections.

## Round 3
Polish all interaction states and compliance details.
Make hover, active, and focus states clearly visible across product cards, bundle controls, buttons, and CTA modules.
Counters should animate into place when scrolled into view, and the sticky navbar should gain a subtle blur, border, or elevation change after scrolling.
Ensure the layout is responsive for desktop and tablet widths while preserving the commerce-first premium look.
Keep all CSS and JS inline inside `index.html`.
Do not use React, Vue, Svelte, jQuery, or any build step.
Do not reference local images, local fonts, local CSS, or local JS.
Use remote imagery only if needed and inline SVG icons.
Return only the final `src/index.html` content with no explanation.

## Round 4
Final refinement pass.
The result should feel premium, useful, and genuinely commerce-ready while still staying visually memorable.
Avoid greenwashing language, empty sustainability claims, and oversized discount-first ecommerce tropes.
Ensure enough depth for a long full-page screenshot and visible interactive states throughout.
Return one complete self-contained `index.html`.
