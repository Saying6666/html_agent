# fdu_026

## Round 1
Create a premium long-scroll single-file `src/index.html` for a **dashboard** called **Petal Brew Club**.
Concept: a luxury tea subscription business expressed through a premium member dashboard that tracks shipments, tasting notes, preferences, and ritual history.
Audience: creative professionals and design-conscious subscribers who want commerce, ritual, and product personalization presented with luxury polish.
Style direction: **Glassmorphism** with references to luxury hospitality pages, cinematic translucent UI, and high-end immersive brand sites.
Component language: translucent panels, blurred overlays, elevated account modules, layered tasting cards, refined preference controls, and premium membership sections.
Color direction: smoky charcoal, soft ivory, misted champagne, silver-blue translucency, and gentle tea-toned highlights.
Typography direction: editorial serif-led headings paired with sophisticated sans-serif body copy.
Motion direction: smooth, ambient, and cinematic with soft depth changes and elegant transitions.
Build a complete page with these sections: sticky navbar, hero summary, membership status, tasting calendar, shipment tracker, flavor preference dashboard, rewards or referral area, FAQ, footer.
Required interactions: hover states, active states, carousel, counters, FAQ accordion.
The page should feel like a premium membership operating system rather than a normal order-tracking account page.

## Round 2
Deepen the layout and content density.
The hero should feel luxurious and intimate, combining elegant product cues with a premium membership dashboard overview.
Add believable copy around shipment timing, tasting journals, flavor history, subscription preferences, referral benefits, and ritual personalization.
Use dashboard storytelling that feels product-real, with preference views, status modules, and member experience moments rather than generic account widgets.
Make the dashboard feel emotionally branded while still useful and clearly navigable.
Keep enough vertical depth for a long full-page screenshot, with multiple strong section transitions and no thin filler sections.

## Round 3
Polish all interaction states and compliance details.
Hover and active states should read clearly across cards, controls, and CTA modules.
Any carousel-style tasting or shipment highlights should feel intentional rather than gimmicky, counters should animate with clean timing, and the FAQ accordion should feel polished and easy to scan.
Ensure the layout is responsive for desktop and tablet widths while preserving the luxury member feel.
Keep all CSS and JS inline inside `index.html`.
Do not use React, Vue, Svelte, jQuery, or any build step.
Do not reference local images, local fonts, local CSS, or local JS.
Use remote imagery only if needed and inline SVG icons.
Return only the final `src/index.html` content with no explanation.

## Round 4
Final refinement pass.
The result should feel luxurious, intimate, and highly product-specific while still remaining easy to use.
Avoid generic tea ecommerce clichés, weak account-page layouts, and decorative glass with no structure.
Ensure enough depth for a long full-page screenshot and visible interactive states throughout.
Return one complete self-contained `index.html`.
